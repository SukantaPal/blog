Making of Diwali Firecrackers

BySukanta Pal 23/10/202127/12/2021

This time of the year when we officially celebrate with firecrackers or crackers(Bazi). Happy to see that now most of the sound firecrackers are very less popular and people also avoid buying them, only celebrating with the lights. Kali Puja, Diwali, Dev Deepawali or any other name you call it; this festival boasts the making of lights, that can be crackers, electric lights or Diya and many more. This is the prime time of selling and manufacturing firecrackers or Bazi market increase. 

Chamahati and Nungi(also Called Nangi) are famous for making firecrackers (Manufacturing hub) of West Bengal. They are making Green Firecrackers for over a decade, due to some restrictions and flood now production very less in terms of the previous year. Champahati’s Haral Village is the maker of crackers when Diwali or kali puja near they sell their crackers roadside temporary shops and also those who have permanent shops sell them also. Those crackers made in Champahati are very cheap but other crackers imported from are not so cheap because those are not produced here. If you want to buy some crackers or wholesale purpose visit Champahati Rail Staton, it’s nearby Champahati Bazi Market.

Nungi is a neighbourhood in the Maheshtala of the South 24 Parganas district in the Indian state of West Bengal. It is a part of the area covered by the Kolkata Metropolitan Development Authority (KMDA). Champahati is a census town and a gram panchayat within the jurisdiction of the Baruipur police station in the Baruipur community development block in the Baruipur subdivision of the South 24 Parganas district in the Indian state of West Bengal. They now make eco-friendly crackers that anyone can buy and easy to sell and top of it environmentally friendly. Slowly now the trend of Sky lantern or fanush flying followed by traditional Diya is more popular when comes to celebrating Diwali or Kali Puja in Kolkata. 

On this occasion of Kali Puja, a few bonedi houses (Bonedi Barir Kali Puja Kolkata or Fanush Bari) of Kolkata or heritage houses of Calcutta flying fanush or hot air balloon in Kolkata (Calcutta). The most popular house is Bholanath Dham Family (33/2, Beadon Street, Kolkata – 700006), after Durga Puja, they started making fanush at their home’s Thakurdalan, it takes time to make because those fanush are big in size also designed fanush lots of paper cutting involved, that’s why it’s very time consuming to make lots of patience and skill is required to make one good fanush or hot air balloon. Every year on the day of Kali Puja Afternoon (around 2-3 pm) they started flying fanush or hot air balloons from their courtyard along with fanush enthusiasts, friends and family; fanush makers are also there for support and help to make it successful. This spectacular fanush flying event was documented by photographers; day by day the number of photographers increase, it’s great to see that fanush flying becoming a trend in Kolkata. 

Started with Fanush Flying event in Kolkata, now Sky lantern or Chinese Lantern are very much popular for eco-friendly and opportunity for great profile picture or Facebook DP, it’s looking good and happy exercise for all of them. Different communities like Photographers, clubs, Residencies or Housing complex people now take it seriously and fanush flying or Sky lantern now trend in Kolkata. In Kolkata we called fanush (Manush Fanush) because it’s started or we know from Bholanath Dham Ajoy Dutt’s house, that’s why that name referred from since then. 

Kolkata Fireworks store or shop previously held at Sahid Minar Ground like fair for selling crackers on The occasion of Diwali and Kali Puja, now happened on Sithir More Circus Ground.
